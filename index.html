How It Works

Identity at
the Turnstile.

MatchPass is a three-layer system: a fan identity credential, a steward-facing PWA, and a cross-club reputation network. All three work together. None of them require the FA's permission.

1
🪪

Fan Gets Their Signet Identity

The fan installs mysignet.app — a self-sovereign identity wallet. They upload a photo, verify basic details, and receive a cryptographic credential. No central database. The fan owns their identity.

2
📱

Steward Scans at the Gate

The fan shows their Signet QR code on their phone (or taps an NFC card or wristband). The steward's app verifies the credential in under 2 seconds: valid ticket, no active ban, photo match. Green light = through. The same credential scanned twice at the same match triggers an automatic card — sharing your pass is a risk to your own record.

3
🌐

Incidents Propagate Across the Network

If a card is issued — yellow warning, red, or full ban — it propagates across every MatchPass club via the Nostr protocol. Cryptographically signed. Tamper-proof. The fan can see their own record. Every card is challengeable.

Accumulation

Three yellow cards trigger an automatic review. A second yellow in the same match is an automatic red. These thresholds are club-configurable. The system enforces them — no admin overhead.

Clean Sheet

Yellow cards expire after 5 clean match appearances. Red cards expire after 10. Fans who stay clean rebuild their reputation automatically. Good behaviour is rewarded. Bad behaviour accumulates.

Challenge Window

Every card is challengeable within 48 hours. The fan receives a notification, can submit their account, and the safety officer must review. All challenges are logged. Appeals are transparent.

Cross-Club Propagation

Bans and suspensions are signed with the issuing club's key and published to the Nostr relay network. Every club on the MatchPass network sees them within seconds. A ban at one club is a ban everywhere on the network.

Scope Options

Suspensions can be home-only, away-only, or all grounds. Clubs set the scope when issuing. The network respects and propagates the scope. A home ban doesn't block away games unless the club specifies it.

Safeguarding

A dedicated safeguarding officer role exists with private case management. Sensitive incidents are not propagated publicly. Only the network-level flag ("safeguarding concern — contact this club") is shared.

Steward · Gate Scanner

Scanning at
the Turnstile

The steward opens the MatchPass gate app on their phone. No login needed on the day — the app stays authenticated via the club's session key. Point, scan, done.

  1. Open MatchPass Gate on your phone — the camera activates automatically
  2. Fan presents their QR code (refreshes every 30 seconds — makes gaming it more effort than it's worth)
  3. Green flash + name = clear to enter. Red = active ban, do not admit
  4. Amber = warning on record — use your judgement, tap for detail
  5. Fan without a phone? Tap NFC from their wristband instead
  6. Offline? The app still works — sanctions sync when signal returns

The entire scan takes under 2 seconds. Your existing turnstile flow doesn't change.

Issuing a Card
in the Stands

An incident happens. The steward doesn't need to radio in or fill out a paper form. They open the card issuer, tap the fan's QR, choose the card, note the reason. Done in 30 seconds.

  1. Tap "Issue Card" in the MatchPass app
  2. Scan the fan's QR or search by name (if they refuse to scan)
  3. Select Yellow or Red — tap for descriptions if uncertain
  4. Choose reason code (threatening language, missiles, hate speech, etc.)
  5. Add a free-text note for context if needed
  6. Confirm — card is issued and the fan is notified immediately
Fan Challenge: The fan receives a notification. They have 48 hours to challenge. All challenges are reviewed by the safety officer and logged.

Getting Started

Live by
Next Matchday.

Five steps. Under an hour. No IT department. Your stewards will be scanning at the gate by Saturday.

1

Create Your Club Account

Go to matchpass.app and register your club. You'll need the club name, your FA registration number, and a contact email. Takes 5 minutes. No payment required.

→ You receive a cryptographic key pair for your club. Keep it safe — it signs all your club's cards.
2

Install the PWA on Steward Devices

Send your stewards the matchpass.app URL. They open it in their browser and tap "Add to Home Screen." It installs like a native app — no app store, no IT approval needed. Works on any phone made in the last five years.

→ Minimum requirement: Android 8+ or iOS 14+, a camera, and a data connection (offline mode kicks in automatically).
3

Add Your Steward Team

In the admin panel, create steward accounts. Assign each steward a role: gate scanner, card issuer, or safeguarding officer. Each role sees only what they need to see. Stewards log in with a 6-digit PIN — no password management required.

→ Bulk import available via CSV if you have an existing steward list.
4

Import Existing Banning Orders

Upload your existing banning order register via the import tool. The system accepts a simple CSV. Once imported, those fans will be flagged automatically at the gate. You can also connect to the Nostr relay to receive cross-club bans from other MatchPass clubs immediately.

→ Import template available in the admin panel. Typical import: under 10 minutes.
5

Go Live on Matchday

Brief your stewards on the app — it takes 5 minutes. Hand them their phones. Open the gate app. The first scan takes about 10 seconds while the steward gets comfortable. By the third fan, it's 2 seconds. By the second match, it's automatic.

→ First-match support available via the matchpass.app community channel.
